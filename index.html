    About

    Abolfazl Eslami is a highly accomplished professor in the Department of Civil and Environmental Engineering at Amirkabir University of Technology (AUT), also known as Tehran Polytechnic, located in Tehran, Iran. With over three decades of experience in the field, Dr. Eslami is a seasoned expert in geotechnical engineering, specializing in special foundations, deep foundations, soil modification and improvement, in-situ testing, bridge engineering, physical modeling via Frustum Confining Vessel (FCV), and database development and implementation focused on Cone Penetration Testing (CPT) and Pile.

    Dr. Eslami received his Bachelor of Science in Civil Engineering from Sharif University of Technology (SUT) in Tehran, Iran, followed by a Master of Science in Geotechnical Engineering from Tehran Polytechnic (AUT). He then pursued a Ph.D. in Geotechnical Engineering from the University of Ottawa in Ottawa, Canada, which he completed in 1997.

    Following the completion of his doctoral studies, Dr. Eslami started his academic career as an Academic Member (Lecturer) in the Civil Engineering Department at the University of Guilan from 1988 to 1992. He then returned to the University of Guilan as an Assistant and Associate Professor from 1997 to 2008. Since 2008, Dr. Eslami has been a professor at Amirkabir University of Technology, where he has taught and mentored countless students.

    In addition to his academic career, Dr. Eslami is also a Senior Engineer and Chairman of Sham-e Consulting Engineering Company, located in Tehran, Iran, where he has been a principal engineer since 2000. He has supervised and participated in many large-scale geotechnical and bridge engineering projects in Iran and other countries.

    Dr. Eslami has been widely recognized for his contributions to the field of geotechnical engineering. He has published numerous research articles in top-tier journals and conferences, and has received many awards and honors, including the Best Professor Award from Amirkabir University of Technology in 2015. His work has made a significant impact on the field, and his research findings have been widely adopted in geotechnical engineering practice.
