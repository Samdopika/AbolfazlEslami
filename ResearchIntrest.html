    Research Intrests

      - Abolfazl Eslami's research interests revolve around various aspects of geotechnical engineering, with a focus on foundation design, soil improvement, in-situ testing, bridge engineering, physical modeling, and database development.

      - Special foundations are designed for specific and challenging soil conditions such as soft soils, expansive soils, collapsible soils, and soils with high water content. Abolfazl Eslami's research interests in this area involve developing effective and efficient special foundation designs that can withstand these difficult soil conditions.

      - Deep foundations are another area of interest for Abolfazl Eslami, and he focuses on the design, construction, and testing of pile foundations, drilled shafts, and other deep foundation systems. He also explores methods to improve their performance and reliability.

      - Soil modification and improvement are essential to enhance the properties of soils and their ability to support structures. Abolfazl Eslami's research interests in this area include developing new methods for soil stabilization, soil reinforcement, and soil densification.

      - In-situ testing, such as Cone Penetration Testing (CPT) and Cone Penetration Testing with pore pressure measurement (CPTu), are crucial for geotechnical design and analysis. Abolfazl Eslami is interested in developing more efficient and effective methods for using these tests in geotechnical practice.

      - Bridge engineering is another area of interest for Abolfazl Eslami, and he focuses on designing foundations for bridges and ensuring their long-term stability and performance.

      - Abolfazl Eslami is also interested in physical modeling, which involves reproducing the behavior of soil and foundation systems under controlled laboratory conditions. His research in this area involves using Frustum Confining Vessel (FCV) physical modeling to improve understanding of the behavior of soils and foundation systems.

      - Finally, database development and implementation, focused on CPT and Pile, is an area of interest for Abolfazl Eslami. He aims to develop databases that can be used to store, manage, and analyze geotechnical data, providing valuable insights for future designs and research.
